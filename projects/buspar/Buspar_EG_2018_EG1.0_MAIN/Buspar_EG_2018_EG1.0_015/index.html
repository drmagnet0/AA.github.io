Buspar (Buspirone): SAFETY GUIDANCE

  • EGY/BUH/0017/17
  • 00120009/010036018D
  • 1/1/2019
  • The maximum daily dosage should not exceed 45 mg per day.
  • Food increases the bioavailability of buspirone; buspirone should be taken at the same time each day and consistently with or without food.
  • If buspirone is administered with a potent CYP3A4 inhibitor, the initial dose should be lowered and only increased gradually after medical evaluation.
  • Grapefruit juice increases the plasma concentrations of buspirone; patients taking buspirone should avoid consuming large quantities of grapefruit juice.
  • Buspirone should be administered with caution and a low dosage, two times daily, in patients with mild-to-moderate renal insufficiency (creatinine clearance 20–49 ml/min/1.72 m2).
  • Buspirone should be used with caution, and individual dosages should be titrated with care in patients with a reduced liver function.
  • Buspirone should not be used in patients hypersensitive to buspirone hydrochloride or any of the excipients, severe renal (creatinine clearance 20 ml/min/1.72 m2 or a plasma creatinine above 200 μmol/l) or severe hepatic insufficiency, acute intoxication with alcohol, hypnotics, analgesics or antipsychotic drugs, patients with epilepsy Buspirone should be used with caution in patients with rare hereditary problems of galactose intolerance, the Lapp lactase deficiency or glucose-galactose malabsorption.
  • It is recommended that buspirone not be used concomitantly with an MAOI.
  • Buspirone should be used with caution in patients with acute narrow-angle glaucoma, myasthenia gravis, drug dependence, and hepatic or renal impairment.
  • Buspirone is not recommended in children and adolescents.
